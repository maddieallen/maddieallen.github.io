Marine Plastic Pollution

Our oceans are dying. What is causing the rapid decline in marine life? What can we do to stop the demise of an important and beloved ecosystem?





What's the Problem with Plastic?

How can a product used daily by millions people around the world be so harmful to our oceans? 

Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels are the building blocks for plastic production. Extracting these releases an immense amount of greenhouse gasses. These gasses trap heat from the sun on the Earth's surface, causing the surface temperature of the ocean to increase as well. This rise in temperature effects the ecosystem as a whole, rendering it unfit for many of the marine life that live near the waters surface. 

Microplastics

Microplastics are extremely small and broken down pieces of plastic that contaminate our oceans. They are often consumed by marine life, releasing harmful toxins into their bodies. 

False Food

A similar affect of microplastics, larger pieces of plastics pose as prey for larger marine animals looking for a meal. Unfortunately for these animals, this plastic pollution is far from food. It results in choking, poisoning, and starving instead of a full belly.

How Can We Help?

There are many ways to help reduce the amount of plastic in our oceans. For starters, we can limit the amount of plastic products we buy. Instead, opt for reusable and recyclable products. Also, refrain from buying products that use or contain microbeads (microplastics), as to reduce the amount of microplastics in the ocean. A good step to take anytime you are at the beach or outside at all is to "take five." Pick up five pieces of plastic or trash before leaving the beach, or a park, or wherever you may be. If nothing else, spreading the word about marine plastic pollution is still a great way to help.
